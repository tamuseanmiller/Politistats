PolitiStats

Unbiased Political Analytics for the Modern Voter.

We believe that political opinions in today's connected world can be swayed by the media and reading what others have to say about politicans. Humans are susceptible to this because of our emotions, but with PolitiStats we wanted to take that aspect out of politics by letting complex algorithms produce sentiment analysis for news and tweets about the politician. This web application was developed using Django and a number of APIs inlcuding the Twitter Developer API, the Google Civic Information API, and the NewsAPI. Our team used the Agile method in the developement process and it proved key to stack on top of the tasks for each sprint. The product allows users to not only understand who represents their state, but what kind of public opinions about them as well. The idea stemmed from the unprecedented 2020 Presidential election and the importance that young voters would play in its outcome. We wanted users to have an experience with that consists of minimal bias and have access to information to help them form their own opinions. All of the data being analyzed is publicly avalible and the algorithms being used are not politically motivated. We also incorporated two features to aid those with visual impairments: a text magnifier, and a light/dark mode toggle switch. In the future, we plan add a map view, search by postal code, tensorflow capabilities, and better integration with mobile devices.
